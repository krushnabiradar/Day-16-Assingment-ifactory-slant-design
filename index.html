THE LONG & THE SHORT OF IT
LONGSCROLL: THE GOOD STUFF
VISUAL CREATIVITY

There is more visual freedom in parallex website design, Rather than
focusing on block style information architecture, you can create
refreshing and encouraging visuals such as animations or beautiful
photography,that acts as a continuum, ultimately inspring users to
scroll further down the page
S
TORY-TELLING
OPPORTUNITIES

Do you want to tell a story with your product or service?
longscrolling lends itself well to a narrative and
encourages meaningful interaction from users.
S
IMPLE INTERFACE FOR
SMART DEVICE USERS

Mobile and tablets have evolved the way we engage in website. It's
now less about scrolling, swiping aand tapping.
Long scrolling websites take away the barrier of complex navigation,
providing a responsive, user friendly experience for mobile users.
P
UNCHY, STRAIGHT TO
THE POINT MESSAGING

Parallex website design is a great solution for us time poor
website browser, as it forces business to convey their
message and vision into one dynamic web design page.
E
ASIER FOR USERS TO
REALISE THEIR GOALS

These days scrolling is so simple that it's become an
instinctive action , where as cliicking through to another page
requires thought and motivation. A single page website
allows users to identify their purpose on the website quicker,
than needing to sift through multiple pages.
LONG SCROLL: THE NOT SO GOOD STUFF
YOU NEED TO BE SEARCH ENGNE SAVVY

One of the biggest problems that parallex websites face is that
they are not seo friendly. Why? when you lmit the number of
pages onn your website, google has fewer paages to index AND
negativly impacts your standing on the search engine results
page (SERP). Luckily there are ways around this.
S
LOWER LOAD TIMES

50% of website users bounce off the page if it hasn't loaded in 3-5 seconds. So the
more high resolution imagery and animations you include, the longer it takes to
load the page. When writing this article, we tested some popular parallax
websiites such that ones with complex visuals took up 30 seconds to
load on a mobile device, where as simple and minimalist long scroll websites
Loaded within a few seconds.
A
WKWARD NAVIGATION

Instead of hitting the back button, users must scroll back
up the page which can be frustrating for users who want to
refer back to certain element. An easy way around this is
to include top navigation and have each element anchored
to a category or section.
